Privacy Policy

Effective date: 13 March 2026  ·  Last updated: 13 March 2026 (added: advisory panel disclosure, OpenRouter routing disclosure)

This policy explains what personal information eskp.in collects when you use the platform, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have over it.

We are subject to the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. Where our users are located in the EU, we also comply with the EU GDPR.

About this service

eskp.in is a peer advisory platform, not a crisis service. Once you are connected with a helper, your conversation takes place directly by email. We are not able to monitor the content of those conversations. If you are in crisis or need urgent support, please contact a specialist service — our support resources page lists free UK services available right now, including Samaritans (116 123, 24/7) and the NHS 111 mental health line. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

1. Who we are

eskp.in is operated by We3.io, based in the United Kingdom. For data protection purposes, we are the controller of your personal data.

We are registered with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller. ICO registration number: C1889388.

Contact us at hello@mail.eskp.in with any privacy-related questions or requests.

2. What we collect and why

When you contact us by email:

  • Your email address
  • The content of your email (your goal, question, or request)
  • Any other information you choose to include

We collect this to provide the core service: understanding what you need and connecting you with someone who can help. The legal basis is legitimate interests — you have contacted us specifically for this purpose.

When we introduce you to a helper:

  • We share your first name (or the name in your email) and a summary of your goal with the helper
  • We do not share your full email address with the helper at this stage
  • If both parties agree to proceed, introductions happen by email with both parties in copy

When you make a payment:

If a helper match is found, we invite you to pay a one-time introduction fee of £10 via Stripe. Payment is handled entirely by Stripe — we never see or store your card details. Stripe may collect your payment card information, billing address, and device metadata in accordance with their own privacy policy.

What we do not collect:

  • We do not use cookies for tracking or advertising
  • We do not collect data from third-party sources

3. AI processing

We use an AI model (Claude Haiku, operated by Anthropic, Inc.) in two steps of the platform's core service:

  1. Goal decomposition — your goal description is analysed to produce a structured breakdown (a summary, a list of specific needs, and context about what success looks like for you).
  2. Helper matching — the structured summary and need tags (not your email address or name) are passed to the AI to rank helpers in our network by relevance.

The legal basis for this AI processing is legitimate interests: you have contacted us specifically to be matched with a helper, and AI processing is integral to how the matching service works.

Data minimisation: we send only your goal text (decomposition) and anonymised need summaries and tags (matching) to Anthropic's API. Your email address and name are never included in API requests.

International transfer: Anthropic, Inc. is based in the United States. API calls are routed through OpenRouter (a US-based API proxy) before reaching Anthropic. Both transfers are covered by standard contractual clauses and data processing agreements. Anthropic's API terms prohibit training on API inputs by default. OpenRouter is configured to opt out of data collection and retention for all our requests.

If you would prefer your goal not to be processed by AI, email us and we will handle it manually. This may take longer.

Sensitive domain review: Goals that touch sensitive domains — such as mental health, domestic abuse, or child safeguarding — may be reviewed by our oversight panel before matching. This is to ensure you receive appropriate signposting to specialist services. Your goal text may be shared with the panel for this purpose. Sensitive goals are not automatically matched.

3a. Peer advisory panel (when enabled)

As an optional feature, users may invite named individuals — friends, colleagues, or others they trust — to participate in a private advisory panel alongside their matched helper. This feature is described here for transparency; it is not yet available to all users.

What this involves:

  • You choose who to invite — we send them an invitation by email with your name and goal summary
  • Invited advisors complete a short onboarding module before joining
  • Advisors can see only the thread for the specific goal they were invited to assist with
  • Advisors cannot see threads from other users, nor other advisors' threads on the same goal

Bilateral isolation design: The platform is designed so that each advisor sees only their own bilateral thread. There is no shared group view. No AI processing of thread content occurs across threads. Advisors do not receive your email address unless you include it in the thread yourself.

Flagging mechanism: If you or an advisor identifies a concern about your wellbeing during the conversation, either party can flag it. A flag notifies our oversight panel and triggers a warm referral to specialist services where appropriate. Flagging is manual — the platform does not monitor conversations automatically.

Data shared with advisors: Your first name, goal summary, and the content of your thread with that advisor. Not your email address (unless you share it), not your payment details, not your threads with other advisors.

Resend (our email provider) processes advisor invitation emails in the same way as other transactional emails — see section 5.

4. How long we keep your data

We keep your data for as long as it is needed to provide the service and for a reasonable period afterwards in case you follow up.

  • Active goals with no activity for 90 days: automatically closed
  • Introduced goals with no activity for 180 days: automatically closed
  • Closed goals: structured data (decomposition) purged after 365 days
  • Backups: overwritten on a 30-day rolling cycle

You can ask us to delete your data at any time — see section 6 below.

5. Who we share your data with

We share your data with:

  • Helpers — people in our network who have agreed to help with goals like yours. We share only what is necessary for the introduction.
  • Resend (resend.com) — our transactional email provider. They process email addresses and message content to deliver emails on our behalf.
  • OpenRouter (openrouter.ai) — an API routing proxy used to forward AI inference requests to Anthropic and other AI providers. OpenRouter processes goal summaries in transit; it is configured to opt out of data collection for all our requests.
  • Anthropic — the AI provider used for goal decomposition and helper matching (see section 3).
  • Stripe, Inc. — our payment processor. When you pay an introduction fee, Stripe receives your payment card details and billing information. We receive only a confirmation of payment. Stripe is based in the US and Ireland and operates under its own privacy policy.
  • Hetzner Online GmbH — our server infrastructure provider, located in the EU/EEA. Data is stored on servers in [data centre location — to confirm].
  • Cloudflare, Inc. — our DNS and edge network provider. Cloudflare processes request metadata to route traffic and protect against abuse.

We do not sell your data. We do not share it with advertisers. We do not use it for any purpose other than providing the service.

6. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Restriction — ask us to restrict processing while a dispute is resolved

To exercise any of these rights, email hello@mail.eskp.in with the subject line "Data request". We will respond within 30 days.

7. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Helpline: 0303 123 1113

We would always prefer to resolve any concern directly — please contact us first.

8. Emergency override (vital interests)

In rare circumstances — where there is an immediate, credible, and specific threat to life and no other protective mechanism is available — we may act on information in a private conversation to protect a vital interest. The legal basis for any such action would be the Data Protection Act 2018, section 15 (vital interests). We would notify our oversight panel immediately and conduct a documented retrospective review.

This is a last resort. The platform does not monitor conversations and has no automated crisis detection. If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, please call 999.

9. Changes to this policy

If we make significant changes, we will note them here with the updated date. For material changes affecting how we use your data, we will notify you by email if we have your address.
