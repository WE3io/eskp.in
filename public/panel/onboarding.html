Advisor Onboarding

Thank you for agreeing to help. Please read the guidance below carefully before accepting.

1. Your role and the dyadic model

You have been invited into a private bilateral relationship with a user. Only you and the user can see your thread — other advisors (if any) have entirely separate threads and cannot see yours.

Your role is: advisor.

2. Crisis recognition and limits

If the user expresses thoughts of suicide or serious self-harm: do not try to handle it alone. Acknowledge what they have shared, stay calm, and provide emergency contacts:

  • Samaritans — 116 123 (free, 24/7)
  • NHS — 111
  • Emergency — 999

Your role is supportive, not clinical. You are not expected to resolve crises.

3. The warm referral protocol

If a topic feels beyond your expertise or comfort: it is always appropriate (and encouraged) to say "I think you'd benefit from speaking to [specialist type]" and to signpost specific services. Never feel obligated to have answers you don't have.

4. Active advice vs witnessed reflection

Witnessed reflection means holding space for someone to think out loud rather than directing them to a conclusion.

Example A

Instead of: "You should leave that job"

Try: "It sounds like this job is draining you. What would it feel like to imagine a different situation?"

Example B

Instead of: "That relationship sounds toxic, end it"

Try: "I hear how much this is affecting you. What do you need right now?"

Practical prompts to use:

  • "What feels most important to you about this?"
  • "What have you already tried?"
  • "What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?"

5. Individual safeguarding obligations

You have a personal responsibility to act if you have a reasonable belief someone is at immediate risk of serious harm. This overrides confidentiality. In the UK, you can contact emergency services directly. You do not need the user's permission. Document what you did and when.

6. How to use "flag for support"

The thread view has a "Flag for support" button. Use it if you are concerned about the user's wellbeing. It sends the user a gentle, non-alarming check-in email from the platform. It does not reveal that you flagged. Use it freely — it is a soft signal, not an alarm.

7. Acknowledgement

"I have read and understood the guidance above. I understand my role, its limits, and my obligations."
