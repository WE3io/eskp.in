# safeguarding-disclosure-terms

**Phase:** 1 — Foundational
**Article:** 11.2.5 (Safeguarding is a shared responsibility)

## Outcome

The terms of service and the helper/panel member onboarding materials explicitly disclose that panel members retain their own individual legal safeguarding obligations and must act on them independently of the platform. The disclosure names the relevant reporting routes (NSPCC, local social care) and makes clear that the platform cannot and does not discharge these obligations on behalf of panel members.

## Constraints & References

- Article 11.2.5: each panel member retains their own legal safeguarding obligations; must be disclosed in onboarding
- Existing: `public/terms.html`, `public/join.html`
- Working Together to Safeguard Children (statutory guidance) — the reporting obligation exists regardless of platform design
- This disclosure applies to panel members / helpers; it is less critical for users but should be present in terms for completeness

## Acceptance Checks

- `public/terms.html` contains a clause stating that helpers/panel members retain individual legal safeguarding obligations
- Clause names NSPCC (0808 800 5000) and local social care as reporting routes for child welfare concerns
- `public/join.html` (helper onboarding page) references the safeguarding obligation explicitly
- Disclosure is in plain English
- Disclosure does not imply the platform will handle safeguarding escalations on behalf of panel members

## Explicit Non-Goals

- Panel member crisis training module (Phase 2 — requires authenticated panel member accounts)
- Technical safeguarding escalation pathway
- Disclosure to end users (this is primarily a helper/panel member obligation)
