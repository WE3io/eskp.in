## Who eskp.in is for

People who are stuck on a specific, concrete goal they haven't navigated before — and who would benefit from a conversation with someone who has.

### Target user backgrounds

| Background | Why they come | Example goal |
|---|---|---|
| Early-career professionals | Career direction, negotiation, first job transitions | "I want to move from backend dev to product management" |
| Solo founders / freelancers | Pricing, positioning, first customers, operational decisions | "I have 40 users and no idea what to charge" |
| Career changers | Navigating unfamiliar industries, transferable skills | "I've been teaching for 10 years, how do I move into UX?" |
| Small business operators | Growth decisions, hiring, process design | "I need to hire my first employee but don't know where to start" |
| People facing practical life decisions | Housing, education, relocation, life transitions | "I'm considering moving abroad for work — what should I think through?" |
| Hobbyists / side-project builders | Technical or creative decisions, getting unstuck | "I'm building a podcast and can't figure out distribution" |

### Common goal characteristics

- **Specific**: not "help me with my life" but "help me decide X"
- **Experience-addressable**: someone who's done this before can meaningfully help
- **Non-regulated**: the goal doesn't require licensed professional advice
- **Time-bounded**: there's a decision or action the person wants to take

## Who eskp.in is NOT for

### Hard exclusions (regulated domains)

These are governed by the [exclusion register](exclusion-register.md). The platform must never market or position itself as serving these needs:

- **Financial advice** (investments, pensions, insurance) — FCA-regulated
- **Legal advice** (reserved activities: litigation, conveyancing, probate) — LSA 2007
- **Immigration advice** (specific individual cases) — IAA 1999
- **Medical diagnosis or treatment** recommendations
- **Psychological therapy** (directing therapeutic techniques)

**Copy rule:** Excluded domains must never appear as use cases, example goals, or enumerated categories on any public-facing page.

### Not a good fit (but not excluded)

- Goals too vague to decompose ("make my life better")
- Needs that require ongoing professional relationships (therapy, accounting)
- Requests for free professional services that should be paid for
- Emergency or crisis situations (these trigger signposting, not matching)

## Goal archetypes

These are the categories the platform actively markets and serves:

| Archetype | Description | Safe to use in copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Career** | Direction changes, negotiation, positioning, industry transitions | Yes |
| **Business** | Pricing, customers, operations, growth decisions | Yes |
| **Technical** | Architecture, stack choices, debugging, system design | Yes |
| **Creative** | Getting unstuck on projects, outside perspective, shipping | Yes |
| **Education** | Learning paths, course selection, self-study strategy | Yes |
| **Life transitions** | Relocation, lifestyle changes, practical planning | Yes |
| **Parenting / family** | Practical decisions (not safeguarding, not therapy) | Yes, with care |

### Boundary cases

| Scenario | Handling |
|---|---|
| "I need legal advice on my contract" | Hard exclusion — warm referral to Citizens Advice / Law Society |
| "I'm thinking about freelancing and wondering about contracts generally" | Acceptable — experience-sharing about freelance setup |
| "I need help with my tax return" | Sensitive handling — general experience OK, specific tax advice signposted |
| "I want to start a business but don't know where to begin" | Core ICP — ideal goal |
| "I'm feeling really low and need someone to talk to" | Sensitive handling — signpost to Samaritans/Mind, panel alert |
| "I want to negotiate a raise" | Core ICP — ideal goal |

## Example guidance for copy

### Do

- Use concrete, specific examples ("negotiate a raise", "choose a tech stack", "price a product")
- Show diverse non-technical goals alongside technical ones
- Frame the platform as experience-sharing, not professional advice
- Use language like "someone who's navigated it before" (not "expert" or "adviser")

### Don't

- List regulated domains as use cases (no "legal", "financial", "medical", "immigration")
- Use "advice" without qualification — prefer "experience", "perspective", "conversation"
- Imply the platform replaces professional services
- Show only tech/startup examples — diversify across archetypes
