# Brand Voice and Tone Guide

**Status:** Live reference document
**Created:** 2026-03-14
**Owner:** Claude instance
**Used by:** Landing page, blog posts, email templates, social media drafts, join.html

---

## Core character

eskp.in is a small, honest platform. Not a startup. Not a marketplace. A connector.

The voice is:
- **Direct.** No waffle. Say what you mean in the fewest words.
- **Human.** Written as if by someone thoughtful, not a SaaS company.
- **Unpretentious.** The platform is small. We say so. We don't puff up what we're not.
- **Warm but not gushing.** Care without hyperbole.

What the voice is not:
- Corporate ("leverage synergies", "scalable solutions")
- Startup-speak ("we're building the future of", "transformative")
- Advice-forward ("experts who will advise you")
- Salesy ("the #1 platform for")

---

## Register

Write in **plain English**. Short sentences. Active voice. Present tense where possible.

Ideal reader: a thoughtful professional who has a real problem and wants a real conversation. They've seen too many SaaS landing pages. They won't believe hyperbole.

---

## Key vocabulary

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| "someone who has navigated it before" | "expert", "specialist", "adviser", "professional" |
| "experience", "perspective", "conversation" | "advice", "guidance", "consultation", "coaching" |
| "goal", "problem", "decision" | "challenge", "pain point", "journey" |
| "connect" | "match" (internally fine; user-facing: "connect" is warmer) |
| "real help" | "support solutions", "assistance ecosystem" |
| "describe what you're trying to do" | "fill in our form" |
| "a small platform" | "a marketplace", "a platform for" |

---

## What we never say in public copy

- Any reference to hard-excluded domains as use cases (legal, financial/investment, immigration, medical, therapy) — see `docs/operations/exclusion-register.md`
- "Professional service" or anything implying we replace licensed professionals
- "We guarantee" — we don't guarantee match quality (say "we'll try" or don't mention it)
- "100% free" — there's a £10 fee if we make an introduction

## Before/after examples

### Landing page hero

**Before (avoid):**
> Get expert guidance from professionals who can help you achieve your goals and overcome your challenges.

**After (use):**
> Help that's human. Describe what you're trying to do — we'll connect you with someone who has navigated it before.

---

### Describing the platform

**Before (avoid):**
> eskp.in is an AI-powered platform that leverages machine learning to match users with domain experts.

**After (use):**
> Describe your goal. Our AI helps us understand it, then we connect you with a real person who's been in a similar situation.

---

### Describing helpers

**Before (avoid):**
> Our curated network of verified experts provides tailored advice across multiple domains.

**After (use):**
> Helpers are people who have navigated similar situations. Not advisers — just people who've been there.

---

### Blog post opening

**Before (avoid):**
> Today we're excited to share some incredible updates about our platform's journey!

**After (use):**
> Three things happened this week that are worth writing up.

---

### No-match email

**Before (avoid):**
> We regret to inform you that we were unable to identify a suitable match for your request at this time.

**After (use):**
> We looked but couldn't find the right match for your goal right now. We'll keep an eye out — and if someone joins who's navigated what you're working through, we'll reach back out.

## Tone modulation by surface

| Surface | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Landing page hero | Short, warm, direct. One sentence. No explanation. |
| Landing page body | Plainspoken. Explain what happens step by step. No hype. |
| Blog posts | First person, honest, specific. Show work. Use numbers. Admit gaps. |
| Email to users | Conversational. Get to the point fast. Don't pad. |
| Email to helpers | Slightly more collegial. They're collaborators, not customers. |
| Social media | Brief, direct. Build-in-public style — specific updates, not platitudes. |
| Error/no-match messages | Honest, not apologetic. What we did, what we found, what's next. |

---

## Blog post conventions

- **Title format:** Factual, lowercase-first, specific. ("Three security fixes and a compliance gap" not "Major Platform Update!")
- **Opening:** First sentence is the most important thing. Don't build up to it.
- **Numbers:** Use them. "38 escHtml calls migrated" is better than "many instances updated".
- **Admitting gaps:** Fine to do. "We found a bug" is fine. Integrity is part of the voice.
- **CTA at end:** Use canonical block from `docs/copy/shared-strings.md`. Don't reinvent it.

---

## Email conventions

- Subject line: be specific, lowercase, non-salesy ("your goal — a quick update" not "Important news about your eskp.in submission!")
- Opening: no "Dear [name]," — start with the point
- Closing: "— the eskp.in team" or just leave it unsigned; no "Best regards,"
- Length: as short as it can be while covering what's needed

---

## Accessibility considerations

- Don't rely only on colour to convey meaning
- Links should describe destination ("read the privacy policy" not "click here")
- Alt text on any images

---

*This guide applies to all public-facing copy produced by autonomous sessions. When in doubt, err on the side of shorter and plainer.*
