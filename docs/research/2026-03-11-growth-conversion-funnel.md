# Growth: Conversion Funnel Review

**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Session:** 30 (self-directed growth rotation)

## Question

What friction exists in the landing page → email → acknowledgement → match conversion funnel? What low-effort improvements can increase discovery and conversion?

## Findings

### 1. SEO basics missing
- No `robots.txt` — search engines may crawl inefficiently
- No `sitemap.xml` — 16 pages not indexed in search engines' sitemap
- No JSON-LD structured data (lower priority; not critical for this type of site)

**Fixed this session:** robots.txt + sitemap.xml created.

### 2. Landing page conversion observations
- CTA is clear and prominent ("Email hello@mail.eskp.in")
- Pre-submission checklist helps quality of submissions
- £10 fee is disclosed early (transparency builds trust)
- "What if there's no match?" section addresses key objection
- "Meet the helpers" section provides social proof
- Page is long (~635 lines of HTML) but well-structured with distinct sections

### 3. Remaining funnel friction (not fixable autonomously)
- **Only 1 helper** — the biggest conversion blocker. Users in non-technical domains will get no match.
- **No analytics** — can't measure where users drop off. Would need Plausible/Fathom/Simple Analytics (privacy-respecting). Requires Sunil to select and configure.
- **No social proof from users** — no testimonials yet (no external users exist)
- **Payment page is Stripe-hosted** — can't customize its messaging. This is fine for now.

### 4. Email flow quality
- Acknowledgement email is well-written (hypothesis framing, reply-if-wrong)
- Follow-up emails exist (24h check-in, 7d no-match, unpaid reminder)
- AI disclosure is present
- Privacy footer is clear
- No identified friction in the email flow itself

## Tasks generated

None — remaining friction is blocked on helper network growth (Sunil) or user volume.

## Relevance

Low-effort SEO basics (robots.txt, sitemap.xml) are done. The conversion funnel is sound for a platform with 1 helper. The binding constraint is supply (helpers), not demand-side friction.
